Since $T$ is holomorphic, its OPE with general field can be expanded in Laurent series. The singular part is
$$
T(z){\cal O}(w,\bar w)=\sum_{n=0}^\infty
\frac{1}{(z-w)n+1}{\cal O}^{(n)}(w,\bar w).
$$
Using radial quantization, the infinitesimal transformation generated by $T$ is then:
$$
\delta_\epsilon{\cal O}(w, \bar w)=\sum_n\frac{1}{n!}\left[
(\partial^n\epsilon){\cal O}^{(n)}(w, \bar w)
\right].
$$
**Primary field** or **tensor operator** with **conformal weights** $(h, \bar h)$ (sometimes referred to as **conformal dimensions**) is an operator which transforms by generalization of metric tensor:
$$
{\cal O}(z, \bar z)\rightarrow{\cal O}'(z',\bar z')=
\left(\frac{\partial z'}{\partial z}\right)^{h}
\left(\frac{\partial\bar z'}{\partial\bar z}\right)^{\bar h}
{\cal O}(z',bar z').
$$
$h+\bar h$ is the **total dimension** and $h-\bar h$ is **spin**.
Infinitesimal transformation is:
$$
\delta_{\epsilon,\bar\epsilon}{\cal O}(z,\nar z)=\left[
(h\partial\epsilon+\epsilon\partial)+
(\bar h\bar\partial\bar\epsilon+\bar\epsilon\bar\partial)
\right]{\cal O}(z,\bar z).
$$
It implies that the OPE reduces to:
$$
T(z){\cal O}(w,\bar w)=\frac{h}{(z-w)^2}{\cal O}(w,\bar w)+
\frac{1}{z-w}\partial{\cal O}(w, \bar w).
$$
For the scalar field, $\partial X(z)$ has weights $(1,0)$, $\bar\partial\bar X(\bar z)$ has $(0,1)$, and $:e^{i\alpha X(z,\bar z)}:$ has $(\frac{\alpha^2}{2},\frac{\alpha^2}{2})$.

Formula for **Schwarzian derivative**:
$$
\{f, z\}=
\frac{2\partial^3f\partial f-3\partial^2f\partial^2f}{2\partial f\partial f}
$$
